Introduction

Our project aims to apply Kripke’s epistemic logic to Cheryl’s birthday puzzle. This puzzle was initially seen at the Singapore and Asian Schools Math Olympiad [1].

The general setting of the puzzle includes 3 people: Albert, Bernard, and Cheryl. It is common knowledge that everyone tells the truth and is a perfect logician. Albert and Bernard need to determine when Cheryl’s birthday is, and she initially gives them 10 possible dates: May 15, May 16, May 19, June 17, June 18, July 14, July 16, August 14, August 15, and August 17. Cheryl then tells Albert the month and Bernard the day. Albert and Bernard then make the following public announcements:

Albert: “I don’t know when Cheryl’s birthday is, but I know that you (Bernard) don’t know either.”
Bernard: “At first I didn’t know when Cheryl’s birthday is, but now I know.”
Albert: “Now I also know when Cheryl’s birthday is.”

The puzzle then asks us to determine Cheryl’s birthday based on the aforementioned knowledge ex- change between agents [1].

The structure of our website is as follows

The tab 'Introduction' introduces the puzzle as it is classically known.

The tab 'Example' shows a derivation of the puzzle in logic notation.

In the tab 'Visual' the Kripke model and it's updates can be seen visually.

In the Visual, you can load the classical model (with the 'Preset' button), set the true state (click on 16 July, then click on the button 'Set'), and go through the steps one by one (the button 'Next' at the bottom).

In the Visual you can also create your own model by adding dates of your choice. It's also possible to load a set of ten random states with the button `Random'. Click 'Clear' to clear all the states.

Conclusion

We researched for what set of dates the puzzle would unfold according to the same story line. We looked all the possible sets that one can create using 10 different dates. We found that there are certain conditions for the public announcements to hold. Our application shows this in a visual, where you can pick any set of dates and see whether the puzzle unfolds with the public announcements from the original example.

Future work

The original puzzle ends where the observer also finds out what the answer is. We did not formulate this into a restriction onto the model. This is something that still could be looked into.

Future research could also be done into considering the sequel of Cheryl's birthday puzzle where there is a third agent who is told Cheryl's birth year.
