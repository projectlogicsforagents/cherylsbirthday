Introduction

Our project aims to apply Kripke’s epistemic logic to Cheryl’s birthday puzzle. This puzzle was ini- tially seen at the Singapore and Asian Schools Math Olympiad [1].

The general setting of the puzzle includes 3 people: Albert, Bernard, and Cheryl. It is common knowl- edge that everyone tells the truth and is a perfect logician. Albert and Bernard need to determine when Cheryl’s birthday is, and she initially gives them 10 possible dates: May 15, May 16, May 19, June 17, June 18, July 14, July 16, August 14, August 15, and August 17. Cheryl then tells Albert the month and Bernard the day. Albert and Bernard then make the following public announcements:

Albert: “I don’t know when Cheryl’s birthday is, but I know that you (Bernard) don’t know either.”
Bernard: “At first I didn’t know when Cheryl’s birthday is, but now I know.”
Albert: “Now I also know when Cheryl’s birthday is.”

The puzzle then asks us to determine Cheryl’s birthday based on the aforementioned knowledge ex- change between agents [1]
